Professor Wangari Maathai

Environmentalist and Nobel Peace Prize Winner

Wangari Maathai was a Kenyan environmentalist, political activist, and the first African woman to receive the Nobel Peace Prize in 2004. She founded the Green Belt Movement, which focused on environmental conservation, tree planting, and women's empowerment. Her dedication to protecting nature and promoting human rights inspired millions around the world.

Some Facts About Her

  • Founded the Green Belt Movement in 1977.
  • Won the Nobel Peace Prize in 2004.
  • Planted millions of trees across Kenya.
  • Advocated for democracy and human rights.
  • Served as Assistant Minister for Environment in Kenya.
  • Inspired environmental conservation worldwide.
  • Became the first African woman to win the Nobel Peace Prize.

Famous Quote

"It's the little things citizens do. That's what will make the difference. My little thing is planting trees."
